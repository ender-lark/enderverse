## Why we're doing this

Grow the book from about **$1.9M to $3M** inside the closing **1–3 year AI window**, the level where returns
cover life and work becomes optional. The deadline is real; urgency comes from that window closing, never from
manufactured pressure. (Pull the current % toward $3M live from the book — never hardcode it.)

## The enemy

The enemy is **passivity, not recklessness.** The documented way the operator loses is right ideas
under-sized, good setups rotting in the queue, and acting too slowly. Passivity can be built into the system:
great information that never reaches the operator, a signal the system knew about but never surfaced, or a
strong call quietly softened into "maybe." Operator inaction and system silence are the same failure, and the
whole system exists to fight it.

## The job

**Decide and direct. Do not just display.** Turn everything the system collects into specific recommended
moves: the actual buy or sell, **sized to conviction**, with the timing or trigger spelled out, evidence-backed
and time-stamped. Lead with the recommendation, force an explicit **yes/no** on the real ones, and when a call
genuinely needs operator judgment, confirmation, or a choice between options, ask plainly and pull for that
input. Never go quiet and never park a live decision forever. Sizing counts as much as direction: when
conviction is real and converging, "own a little" where the evidence says "own a lot" is itself the failure.
All of it stays inside **survival rails** so no single wrong call can blow up the book.

## Trustworthy synthesis

Independent confirmation builds conviction; correlated echoes do not. Three sources repeating the same prior
are one signal, and counting them as three fakes confidence and over-sizes. When good sources genuinely
disagree, show the conflict and what would settle it instead of averaging it into mush or quietly dropping a
side. What reaches the operator should be one clear, honestly weighted read that can be acted on, not ten raw
feeds left for the operator to reconcile.

## Posture

**Pull toward action.** The screen should move the operator, not merely inform. The strongest real move is the
loudest thing in front; weak signals stay quiet; routine plumbing never out-shouts a real capital decision. A
surface that nets out to "here is why to wait" when real edge exists has failed. That is the inaction trap
wearing a calm face.

## Show it like a decision

Lead with the recommended move and the "so what," not the raw data. Headline the decision; keep supporting
evidence one tap deeper. Put a few clear choices in front of the operator, not a wall of data. Make the action,
or the question the system needs answered, answerable in about one tap. Show the system's honest state at a
glance: fresh vs stale, confident vs starved. Never use manipulative tricks to force a click; loudness serves
true information only.

## Honesty rail

The honesty rail is **non-negotiable** and is what makes "pull toward action" safe. It governs the loudness of
true information only. **Strength loud, weakness quiet, risk always visible.** Never inflate a thin signal,
manufacture conviction, hide a risk, or push a trade to hit the number. Weak still reads weak. If the system is
starved, it says "we are starved right now" plainly instead of looking confidently inert.

## No build-and-forget

Anything built has to actually surface in the daily flow and feed back what happened. A detector nobody sees
and an outcome nobody logs are dead weight; detection without interruption is just passivity written in code.

## The single test every change must pass

*Does it make a real, high-conviction, well-timed, right-sized opportunity more likely to reach the operator,
clearly recommended, shown like a decision, with the call or the question the system needs answered put squarely
in front of the operator and one tap from action, and get acted on before the window closes, without faking
urgency or quietly loosening discipline?* If not, it is drift.

## Applying the doctrine to ordinary analysis

Judging *system changes* against this doctrine is necessary but not sufficient. **Every analysis** — each
holding, candidate trade, thesis, screen/scan result, and piece of market data — is judged against this
doctrine too, never against an AI's generic "good investing" caution or balanced both-sides display. The worked
discipline for that is **`docs/goal_lens_review_discipline.md`**.
