# Research 01 — Target Business Selection & Economics

> Basis for the **prospect scoring + willingness-to-pay engine** (`docs/plan/03-prospect-engine.md`). Synthesized from ~23 per-vertical deep-dives (2024–2026 sources; full source URLs preserved in the session research log). **Caveat:** LTV/CAC/CPL figures are largely marketing-agency/vendor benchmarks (directional, not audited); revenue/margin anchors lean on IBISWorld, ADA HPI, AVMA, AmSpa, NAHB where available.

## The core thesis

The ideal prospect maximizes four things at once:
1. **High value per customer** (LTV) — so one extra customer pays for the website many times over.
2. **High dependence on local online discovery** — so a website + Google presence actually moves revenue.
3. **Enough business volume / margin** — so they can comfortably afford a premium monthly fee.
4. **Winnability** — strong reviews (proven demand) but NO website (the gap we close).

A business that is well-reviewed but website-less is *already proving demand by word-of-mouth*; the pitch is pure **capture**, framed by **loss aversion** (see Research 03).

### Practical targeting guidance
- **Default A-tier focus:** med spa, dental/ortho, dermatology, cosmetic surgery, fertility — high LTV + cash-pay + image-sensitive + strong margins = highest willingness-to-pay and easiest ROI story.
- **High-ticket trades (B):** roofing, HVAC, remodeling, pool, windows — one job dwarfs the website cost, so the ROI math in the pitch is overwhelming.
- **Volume plays (B/C):** plumbing, electrical, auto, pest, PT, chiro, vet, salons — abundant prospects, mid WTP; good for filling a pipeline.
- **De-prioritize:** restaurants (margin/churn), solar (2026 headwinds) unless a specific strong prospect appears.

## Universal facts that power the pitch (true across verticals)
- ~46% of all Google searches have local intent; the **Map 3-Pack appears on ~93% of local-service searches** and captures the large majority of clicks.
- **76–88%** of "near me" / local-mobile searches lead to a call or visit within 24 hours.
- A **complete Google Business Profile gets ~7× more clicks**; a website is what converts that interest.
- **~27% of small businesses still have no website (2026)** — the prospect pool is real and large.
- Healthy **LTV:CAC ≥ 3:1** is the universal benchmark; for A-tier verticals it's frequently 10–50:1, meaning a website that yields even 1–2 extra customers/month is wildly net-positive for the owner.

## Willingness-to-pay model (inputs → monthly price)

The price a prospect will plausibly pay scales with (a) category LTV, (b) local market wealth, (c) review strength/credibility, and (d) competitive pressure. This feeds the pricing engine in Research 02 and `docs/plan/03-prospect-engine.md`:

```
monthly_price ≈ BASE × industry_multiplier × geo_multiplier × strength_multiplier
```
- **BASE** = $199/mo (Growth tier anchor, per Research 02).
- **industry_multiplier** (0.8–2.0): driven by category LTV/margin — A-tier medical/legal ≈ 1.8–2.0; high-ticket trades ≈ 1.3–1.5; standard services ≈ 1.0–1.2; low-ticket personal care ≈ 0.8–1.0.
- **geo_multiplier** (0.85–1.40): from zip median household income (Census ACS) vs national median.
- **strength_multiplier** (≈0.9–1.15): more reviews / higher rating / strong competitor pressure = more they'll pay to capture demand they're already generating.
