# Small-Business Website Pricing Brief (US, 2025–2026)

**Purpose:** Build a real pricing engine for a salesperson selling premium websites — plus ongoing hosting, maintenance, and lead generation — to local small businesses that currently have **no website**. These owners are non-sophisticated buyers: they don't comparison-shop on token costs or hosting margins; they buy outcomes (more calls, more bookings) and trust/credibility. That changes what's chargeable.

**Bottom line up front:**
- Recommended core offer: **$0–$500 down + a 12-month "Website-as-a-Service" plan** that bundles build + hosting + maintenance + lead-gen, then converts to a lower ongoing care/lead-gen plan in year 2+.
- Recommended base plan price: **~$199/mo (12-mo min)** for a standard local service business, scaled by **industry multiplier (0.8×–2.0×)** and **geographic multiplier (0.85×–1.4×)**.
- True cost to deliver is **~$15–$40/mo per site** plus a few hours of (largely AI-assisted) build labor, so gross margins on the monthly plan run **80–90%+**.

## 2. Pricing Models — Which One to Use

| Model | How it works | Pros | Cons | Fit for non-sophisticated local owner |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **One-time project fee** | $3k–$8k upfront, done | Big cash now | Sticker shock kills no-website prospects; no recurring revenue; churn at handoff | Poor |
| **One-time + monthly care plan** | $2k–$5k build + $50–$300/mo | Recurring revenue; standard agency model | Upfront still a barrier; care plan seen as "optional" and gets cancelled | Medium |
| **Pure monthly subscription / Website-as-a-Service (WaaS)** | $0–low down + **$X/mo for 12–24 mo**, bundles build+host+maintain+leads | **Removes upfront barrier** (biggest objection for no-website owners); high retention via contract + bundled value; predictable MRR; you own the site until paid | Lower first-year cash; need contract discipline; some "$99/mo" providers get a bad rep for nickel-and-diming | **Best** |
| **Productized / value-based** | Fixed-scope niche packages priced to outcome, sold as flat monthly | Repeatable delivery; premium pricing; easy to scale with templates/AI | Requires niching down | **Best (combine with WaaS)** |

**Why subscription/WaaS wins for this buyer:**
- The #1 objection from a business with *no* website is the upfront check. WaaS converts "$5,000 now" into "$199/mo" — a no-brainer operating expense framed against the value of even one new customer.
- Recurring-revenue businesses have historically grown ~**5× faster** than the S&P 500; recurring models smooth cash flow between builds and keep you in monthly contact (driving referrals and upsells) which **increases retention**.
- Productized + value-based pricing lets you charge on *outcome* (leads/credibility) rather than hours, and template/AI-driven delivery keeps margins high.

**Recommendation:** Sell a **productized Website-as-a-Service** — bundle build + hosting + maintenance + lead-gen into a single monthly price on a **12-month minimum term** (offer 24-month at a discount), with a small or $0 setup fee to clear the upfront barrier. After the initial term, drop to a lower **ongoing care + lead-gen plan** so the relationship (and MRR) continues.

Sources: [SuperDupr – subscription web design](https://superdupr.com/blog/subscription-web-design-services), [Wix Studio – recurring revenue](https://www.wix.com/studio/blog/recurring-revenue-for-agencies), [Kinsta – recurring revenue](https://kinsta.com/blog/build-recurring-revenue-into-agency/), [Dorik – productized web design](https://dorik.com/blog/how-to-start-productized-web-design-business), [Knapsack Creative – pricing](https://knapsackcreative.com/blog/web-design/web-design-pricing). Note: subscription guides warn against "$99–$299/mo" offers that cap revisions and push out-of-scope upgrades — set generous-but-bounded scope to avoid that reputation.

## 3. Variation by Business Type (Industry Multipliers)

Price tracks the **customer lifetime value** of the prospect's own customers. A law firm or med spa client is worth thousands per lead and will pay far more than a barbershop.

Observed market anchors:
- **Law firm:** solo/small $3,000–$10,000 build; mid/large $8,000–$20,000+; maintenance $500–$6,000/yr.
- **Dental:** typical agency quote **$10,000–$15,000**.
- **Medical / med spa:** small practice $4,000–$8,000; custom/multi-location $8,000–$20,000+; budget template builds from ~$2,000.

Sources: [PaperStreet – law firm pricing](https://www.paperstreet.com/blog/pricing-for-law-firm-web-sites/), [Grow Law](https://growlaw.co/blog/law-firm-website-design-pricing), [12AM Agency – dental](https://12amagency.com/blog/dental-website-design-cost-in-2025-a-complete-price-guide/), [Flamingo – medical](https://www.flamingoagency.com/blog/medical-website-design-cost/), [SERPreme – med spa](https://serpremeseo.com/services/medical-spa-web-design/).

### Suggested industry tier table (multiplier applied to base monthly price)

| Tier | Industries | Industry multiplier |
|---|---|---|
| **A – Premium / high-LTV regulated** | Law firms, dentists, med spas, cosmetic/derm, plastic surgery, financial advisors, private medical | **1.8×–2.0×** |

## 4. Variation by Geography (Geographic Multipliers)

Geography moves freelance/agency rates by **2–4×** across US markets. Two public datasets drive a defensible multiplier:

1. **BEA Regional Price Parities (RPP):** national avg = 100. Highest states 2024: California **110.7**, Hawaii **110.0**, New Jersey **108.8**. Lowest: Arkansas **86.9**, Mississippi **87.0**, Iowa/Oklahoma **87.8**. At metro level the spread is wider (e.g., $100 buys ~$84.58 of goods in San Francisco vs ~$124.49 in Pine Bluff, AR).
2. **Census median household income (MHI):** US median ≈ **$81,600 (2024 ACS)**. Affluent suburbs run **5–7× the national median** (Scarsdale NY avg household income ~$612k; West University Place TX ~$440k).

Sources: [BEA – RPP](https://www.bea.gov/data/prices-inflation/regional-price-parities-state-and-metro-area), [Tax Foundation – purchasing power](https://taxfoundation.org/data/all/state/purchasing-power-real-value-100/), [Census – Household Income 2024](https://www.census.gov/library/publications/2025/acs/acsbr-025.html), [Yahoo – wealthiest suburbs](https://finance.yahoo.com/real-estate/articles/10-wealthiest-suburbs-america-091047824.html), [Upwork – freelance rates](https://www.upwork.com/resources/how-to-set-your-freelance-rate).

### Recommended geographic multiplier (drive off zip-code median household income)

Use zip MHI as the primary signal (it directly reflects the buyer's ability to pay and their customers' wealth), with RPP as a secondary sanity check. Bands relative to ~$82k national median:

| Zip MHI band | Market description | Geo multiplier |
|---|---|---|
| < $50k | Rural / low-income | **0.85×** |
| $50k–$70k | Below-avg metro / small town | **0.95×** |
| $70k–$95k | Around national median | **1.00×** |
| $95k–$130k | Above-avg suburb / strong metro | **1.15×** |
| $130k–$180k | Affluent suburb | **1.30×** |
| > $180k | Wealthy enclave (Scarsdale-tier) | **1.40×** |

> Implementation: at quote time, look up the prospect's zip MHI (Census ACS API, free) → map to band → multiplier. Optionally floor/cap with the state RPP so you don't overprice a high-income household in a genuinely cheap metro.

## 5. Recommended Price Book for THIS Operator

### Base plan (Tier C standard business, national-median zip = 1.0× / 1.0×)

| Item | Setup (one-time) | Monthly (12-mo term) |
|---|---|---|
| **Starter** (5-page site, hosting, maintenance, GBP setup) | $0–$299 | **$149/mo** |
| **Growth (BASE)** (above + on-page SEO, lead forms/call tracking, monthly report) | $0–$499 | **$199/mo** |
| **Pro** (above + active local SEO/lead-gen, content updates, booking integration) | $0–$999 | **$349/mo** |

Year-2+ conversion: drop to **$79–$149/mo** care + lead-gen retainer (keeps MRR, lowers churn since build cost is amortized).

### Quote formula
```
Monthly = BASE_monthly ($199) × Industry multiplier × Geographic multiplier
Setup   = optional, $0–$999 (use $0 to win, or small setup to qualify seriousness)
12-mo contract value = Monthly × 12 + Setup
```

### Worked example quotes

| Prospect | Base | Industry mult | Geo mult (zip MHI) | **Monthly** | 12-mo value |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **4.8★ med spa**, affluent suburb (MHI ~$140k) | $199 | 1.9× (Tier A) | 1.30× | **$199 × 1.9 × 1.30 = ~$492/mo** | ~$5,900 + setup |
| **4.9★ dentist**, wealthy suburb (MHI ~$185k) | $199 | 1.9× (Tier A) | 1.40× | **$199 × 1.9 × 1.40 = ~$529/mo** | ~$6,350 + setup |
| **4.6★ plumber**, mid-income metro (MHI ~$78k) | $199 | 1.4× (Tier B) | 1.00× | **$199 × 1.4 × 1.00 = ~$279/mo** | ~$3,350 + setup |
| **4.7★ HVAC**, above-avg suburb (MHI ~$110k) | $199 | 1.4× (Tier B) | 1.15× | **$199 × 1.4 × 1.15 = ~$320/mo** | ~$3,840 + setup |
| **4.5★ barbershop**, rural town (MHI ~$48k) | $199 | 0.9× (Tier D) | 0.85× | **$199 × 0.9 × 0.85 = ~$152/mo** | ~$1,830 + setup |

Round to clean numbers (e.g., $492 → **$499/mo**; $152 → **$149/mo**). Anchor with the Pro tier, sell the Growth tier, keep Starter as the "down-sell" so no prospect walks.

**Sanity check vs. market:** A med spa at $499/mo × 12 = ~$6k/yr is below a single one-time custom med-spa build ($8k–$20k+) yet includes hosting, maintenance, and lead-gen — easy to justify. A plumber at $279/mo is in line with the $500–$1,000/mo small-business SEO band while *also* delivering the site. None of these prices are out of market; they're framed to feel small monthly.

## 6. Cost to Deliver (Margin Model)

### Per-site recurring cost

| Cost item | Monthly | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Hosting (managed, or shared/VPS amortized across many sites) | $3–$20 | Budget shared hosting $2–$10; managed WP ~$20. Bulk/multi-tenant lowers this. |
| Domain | ~$1–$2 | $10–$20/yr amortized |
| SSL | $0 | Let's Encrypt free |
| Call tracking / lead form / analytics tooling | $5–$15 | Optional add-on tools |
| AI/API (content, updates, build assist) | $1–$10 | See below |
| **Total recurring per site** | **~$15–$40/mo** | |

### One-time build labor (AI-assisted)
- AI generation is cheap: Claude Haiku **$1/M input, $5/M output**; Grok 4.1 **$0.20/M in, $0.50/M out**; GPT-4o **$5/M in, $15/M out**. A full site build pushes well under **$5–$30 of tokens** even with heavy iteration; light jobs are pennies. Subscription dev tools ($20/mo) cover most volume.
- Real cost is **operator time**: with templates + AI, a standard local site is a few hours of setup/customization/content. Even at a $75/hr opportunity cost, that's **~$150–$450 of labor**, fully recovered inside the first 1–3 monthly payments.

Sources: [Claude pricing](https://claude.com/pricing), [morphllm – AI coding costs](https://www.morphllm.com/ai-coding-costs), [IntuitionLabs – AI API pricing](https://intuitionlabs.ai/articles/ai-api-pricing-comparison-grok-gemini-openai-claude), [Hostinger – WordPress pricing](https://www.hostinger.com/tutorials/wordpress-pricing).

### Margin illustration (Growth tier, standard plumber @ $279/mo)
- Revenue: **$279/mo** → $3,348/yr.
- Recurring delivery cost: **~$25/mo** → $300/yr.
- Amortized build labor: ~$300 one-time.
- **Year-1 gross margin ≈ ($3,348 − $300 − $300) / $3,348 ≈ 82%.** Year-2+ (no build cost) ≈ **91%**.
- Med spa @ $499/mo: year-1 gross margin **~88%**, year-2+ **~94%** (delivery cost is roughly fixed regardless of price → premium tiers are pure margin expansion).

## Key Takeaways for the Pricing Engine

1. **Lead with monthly, not upfront.** $0–$500 down + $X/mo for 12 months removes the deal-killing objection for no-website owners.
2. **Price = BASE ($199) × Industry (0.8–2.0×) × Geography (0.85–1.4×).** Both multipliers are data-driven (industry tier table + zip-code median household income).
3. **Premium niches (law/dental/med spa) and affluent zips are where the money is** — same delivery cost, ~2–2.5× the price, near-pure-margin.
4. **Delivery cost is ~$15–$40/mo + a few hours of AI-assisted build**, so every tier clears 80–94% gross margin; the constraint is sales/throughput, not COGS.
5. **Convert to a year-2 care+lead-gen retainer** ($79–$149/mo) to lock in long-term MRR and retention.
