# Research 03 — The Cold Pitch / Sales Playbook

> Source: dedicated research stream (2024–2026 sources). This is the basis for the **pitch generator** (`docs/plan/04-pitch-generator.md`). Audience for the generated artifact: people-skilled callers who are NOT marketers — output must be simple, skimmable, partly verbatim-speakable.

## 0. Strategic premise — why this exact segment converts

The ideal prospect is a business with **strong reviews + NO website**. That combination *is* the pitch:

- They've already proven demand and quality — reviews solve "is this business good?", so the pitch is purely about **capture**, not persuasion of quality.
- **97%** of people search online before visiting a local business; **81%** research online before purchasing — so a no-website business is invisible at the moment a referred customer verifies them. (Map Labs; Marketing LTB)
- A no-website local business is estimated to **lose 20–35% of referred customers** at the "verification" step. (LeadsAgent)
- **69%** of consumers say a website is essential for local-business credibility; **75%** judge credibility on the website; **74%** of 18–24s prefer businesses with a website. (DreamHost 2026 Trust Index; Made For Web)
- **~27%** of small businesses still have no website in 2026 — these prospects exist in volume and usually have a competitor who *does* have one. (LeadsAgent; b2bleadfinder)

**Psychology = loss aversion, not gain.** People feel a loss ~2x more than an equivalent gain. The winning frame: *"You're already winning — and leaking the customers your reputation earned you."* (Amplemarket; Gong; Sybill)

## 1. Cold-call structure (the proven skeleton, ~60s to first response)

**Hook (~15s) → Bridge (~15s) → Reason/Value (~20s) → Soft Ask (~10s).** A flexible framework, NOT a teleprompter.

| Stage | Function |
|---|---|
| Opener + pattern interrupt | Name + company, own the interruption ("I know I'm calling out of the blue") |
| Permission ask | "Can I borrow 27 seconds to tell you why I called, then you decide?" (oddly-specific number = pattern interrupt) |
| Relevance hook / value | ONE specific prospect-true fact (their stars + no website) |
| One discovery question | Light SPIN, not interrogation |
| Objection handling | Acknowledge → reframe → re-ask |
| Close = BOOK, don't sell | Offer two specific follow-up times |

**Benchmarks:** baseline cold-call success ~2.3% (2025); structured + targeted lists 5–8%. 3 min of research → +82% conversion (this tool *is* that research, pre-done). Best windows: Tue–Thu, 10am–12pm & 2pm–4pm local. Call + same-day email → ~70% lift.

## 2. Value props, ranked by persuasiveness (this segment)

1. **"Invisible despite great reviews" gap** (lead with this) — "4.8 stars, 180 reviews, but when someone Googles you there's nothing to land on."
2. **Named competitor is catching your customers** (FOMO/social proof) — see §3.
3. **Lost-customer math** — quantify the leak (20–35% of referred customers drop at verification).
4. **Show up in Google Search & Maps** — "near me" searches +400% since 2020; 76% of nearby searchers visit within 24h.
5. **Capture after-hours leads** — site books 24/7.
6. **Credibility for younger customers** — 74% of 18–24s prefer businesses with a website.
7. **You own it** (vs rented social) — mostly a rebuttal to the Facebook objection.

## 3. Competitor framing (the single most powerful device)

Naming a real local competitor who HAS a strong site converts "you should have a website" into "**a specific rival is taking your customers right now.**" Data points to pull about the competitor:

- Their Google ranking for "[service] + [town]" / "[service] near me" (e.g. "#1–3").
- Their review count & stars — **especially if the prospect's are BETTER** (the kicker).
- They have a website (the asymmetry).
- Optional: est. monthly search volume for the core keyword; "appears in the Map Pack."

**Rule:** name them once, factually, no trash-talk. Let the asymmetry do the work.

## 4. Objection handling (acknowledge → reframe → re-ask)

| Objection | Rebuttal |
|---|---|
| Too busy / enough work | "Exactly why I called — this isn't more work for you; it's so the customers you already earn can find and book you. Slow-season insurance." |
| I have a Facebook page | "Great supplement, but it's co-branded, shows competitors' ads, and won't rank you on Google. Your site is yours, on your domain." |
| Too expensive | "Fair. If even one or two extra customers a month come from it, it pays for itself — and you're losing ~1 in 4 referred people now. Fixed package + installments." |
| Tried before / didn't work | "Usually that's a generic site nobody could find. I build around the searches your customers actually type and tie it to your Google listing. Can I show you for *your* business?" |
| Send me an email | "Happy to — but owners skim and forget. Let's grab 15 min Thursday so I can show you the 2 searches you're missing. Morning or afternoon?" |
| Not interested | "No problem — quick thing: [Competitor] shows up #1 for '[service] near [town]' even though your reviews are better. That's why I called. Worth 15 minutes?" |

## 5. The pitch artifact spec (the generated one-pager)

**Three zones, top→bottom. Bullets not paragraphs; every number pre-filled; bold the speakable lines.**

**Header:** `prospect_name`, `category`, `town/area`, `phone`, `best_call_window` (default Tue–Thu 10–12 / 2–4 local).

**(A) Read-aloud script — 5–7 verbatim bullets:**
1. `opener` — name + company + own-the-interrupt
2. `permission` — "27 seconds, then you decide"
3. `hook` — their stars/reviews + "nothing to find when people Google you"
4. `competitor_jab` — named competitor ranks #1 despite weaker reviews
5. `loss_math` — "you're likely losing ~X customers/month at the moment they look you up"
6. `discovery_q` — one light SPIN question
7. `close` — book a 15-min follow-up, **two specific times**

**(B) Key-facts cheat-sheet (scan in 2s):** `stars`, `review_count`, `category`; `competitor_{name,rank,stars,reviews,has_site}`; `core_keyword`; `roi_math` (est_monthly_searchers → est_lost_pct 20–35% → est_lost_customers/mo → avg_ticket → est_monthly_revenue_at_risk); `recommended_offer` (package, one-time price, monthly, payment-plan note).

**(C) Deeper section (skimmable, one line each):** `top_3_objections` (always include Busy, Expensive, Facebook) + rebuttals; `offer_detail` (mobile site, GBP setup/link, contact+booking, local SEO for core keyword, timeline, risk-reversal); `next_step_email` (one-line same-day follow-up); `sources_line` (1–2 quotable stats).

**Default offer anchor (from pricing research):** SMB sites ~$1,500–$5,000 one-time (avg ~$4,500) + $95–$200/mo care plans; always offer fixed package + installments.

## 6. Worked example (the template, filled)

**PROSPECT:** Lumière Med Spa — Med Spa — Oakhaven (affluent suburb) · (555) 010-2840 · Call Tue–Thu 10–12 / 2–4

**━━ A. READ ALOUD ━━**
1. "Hi, is this the owner of Lumière Med Spa? Hi [Name], this is Sam with [Company] — I know I'm calling out of the blue."
2. "Can I borrow 27 seconds to tell you exactly why I called, and then you can tell me to buzz off if it's not useful?"
3. "You've got 4.8 stars and 180 reviews — better than almost every med spa around you. But when I went to find your website to book… there isn't one. Everyone who hears about you and Googles you hits a dead end."
4. "Meanwhile, Radiance Aesthetics two streets over shows up #1 for 'med spa near Oakhaven' — and they've only got 4.5 stars and 90 reviews. Half your reputation, but they're catching the customers searching for you."
5. "In a town like Oakhaven, you're likely losing around 15–25 high-value bookings a month right when people look you up and can't find you."
6. "Quick question — right now, when someone wants to book you after hours, what do they actually do?"
7. "All I want is 15 minutes to show you the exact two searches you're missing and what fixing it looks like. Thursday at 10, or Thursday at 2?"

**━━ B. KEY FACTS ━━**
- 4.8★ · 180 reviews · Med Spa
- Competitor: **Radiance Aesthetics** — #1 · 4.5★ · 90 reviews · has website
- Core keyword: "med spa near Oakhaven"
- Lost math: ~80 monthly searchers × ~25% lost ≈ ~20 lost/mo × ~$250 ticket ≈ **~$5,000/mo at risk**
- Offer: **"Oakhaven Launch" — $2,400 one-time + $150/mo care.** 3-pay option.

**━━ C. IF THEY PUSH BACK ━━**
- Busy → "Brings booked clients *to* you, not more work."
- Expensive → "One extra client/week pays for it; you're leaking ~$5k/mo. Installments available."
- Instagram/Facebook → "Great supplement, but social won't rank you on Google. The site is yours."
- Includes: mobile site + booking, GBP setup & link, reviews showcase, local SEO for core keyword. Live ~2 weeks.
- Same-day email: competitor's search result vs your empty one + the 2 missing searches + Thursday times.
- If challenged: "97% search online before visiting; 75% judge you by your website."
